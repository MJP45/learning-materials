FAMILY/HERITAGE

I was born and bred in the vibrant streets of Battersea in South London and raised with three brothers by Ghanaian parents. I also have a young niece who I love to bits and is now the little sister I never had. My nickname growing up, given by my siblings, was 'diella the ugly fella' which I absolutely hated!

Fun fact: People would call us the 'D Family' as all of our names begin with a D!

EDU/CATION

As I child I loved and enjoyed reading about anything and everything! I had a fascination with wanting to learn about the world around me and would stay up late learning, reading or watching documentaries with my dad. Thus, when it came to education, although I was quite the class clown, I loved all my subjects (except German!).

Recently, I graduated from the University of Warwick with an MEng in Electronic Engineering, which was a great challenge!

DA/NCE

A main passion of mine began by randomly following my best friend to a ballet class. I always enjoyed dancing and with the various parties held by my extended family, dancing was always prominent both socially and culturally.

Ballet and cultural dancing turned to Hip Hop dance as my favourite style. I have been lucky enough to perform and choreograph in many spaces such as Sadler's Well Theatre, University competitions and charity events.

HAIR/IDENTITY

My hair has always been a large part of my expression throughout different stages of my life. Although there were times I wanted to rip it out of my head, there were others when I marveled at how diverse it was.

I began teaching myself different styles, and learning about how my ancestors used they're hair to present identity in their societies. Whether its braids, my afro, curly or coloured, I can express my creativity.
